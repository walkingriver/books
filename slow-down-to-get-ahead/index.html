Here's the truth nobody tells you: technical skills get you hired. Communication skills get you promoted—or fired.

I Learned This the Hard Way

In 2005, I was a senior developer at HP. I was right about a technical decision. I knew it. My code proved it.

But I said it the wrong way, to the wrong people, at the wrong time. I got put on 12-month probation. I quit on the spot. I didn't work as a software developer again for three years.

That was expensive lesson #1 of many.

Over 30 years at HP, Compaq, Dell, Disney, and startups of all sizes, I've made every communication mistake in the book—and eventually learned what actually works.

This book is 26 chapters of those lessons, written so you don't have to learn them the hard way.
